PM Kisan Samman Nidhi
Yojana

Initiatives by Central Ministries and Departments

Overview

The scheme was first conceived and implemented by government of Telangana as the Rythu Bandhu scheme, where a certain amount is given directly to eligible farmers. The scheme has received accolades from various organisations for its successful implementation, including World Bank. Many economist suggest that this type of investment support is better than farm loan waivers. With the positive outcome of this scheme, Government of India wanted to implement it as nationwide project and it was announced by Piyush Goyal during the 2019 Interim Union Budget of India on 1 February 2019.

Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare has awarded the top-performing states, and districts under PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana. In addition to PMKISAN, individuals can explore a wide range of government schemes aimed at providing financial assistance and support to farmers and other beneficiaries. It is based on the criteria such as correction of data, addressing farmer grievances, and timely physical verification exercise

Ministry of Agriculture and Information Technology

Eligibility Criteria:-

  • Small and marginal farmers who own up to 2 hectares (about 5 acres) of cultivable land.
  • Land ownership records must be maintained by the state/UT government.
  • Farmers must be registered under the scheme and have an Aadhaar card for identification.

Registration Process:-

  • Offline: Farmers can register through their local agriculture office or Common Service Centers (CSCs).
  • Online: Registration can also be done through the official PM-Kisan portal (pmkisan.gov.in) .
  • Documents Required: Aadhaar number, bank account details, and land ownership documents.

Benefits:-

  • Financial Support: Provides a steady source of income for farmers, helping them with crop investment and other agricultural activities.
  • Poverty Alleviation: Aims to reduce poverty among small and marginal farmers by providing them financial stability.
  • Improving Agricultural Productivity: Enables farmers to invest in better seeds, fertilizers, and other inputs, potentially increasing agricultural productivity.

Recent Updates:-

  • The government periodically updates the list of beneficiaries and conducts verification to ensure that only eligible farmers receive the benefits.
  • The scheme's scope and reach are reviewed regularly to address any gaps or issues in implementation.

How to Check Beneficiary Status:-

Farmers can check their beneficiary status on the PM-Kisan official website by entering their Aadhaar number, bank account number, or registered mobile number.
