Market Intervention Scheme and Price Support Scheme (MIS-PSS)

Initiatives by Central Ministries and Departments

Overview

The Market Intervention Scheme (MIS) and Price Support Scheme (PSS) are governmental initiatives designed to stabilize prices and support farmers in times of market distress. These schemes are aimed at providing price stability and preventing price crashes in agricultural commodities, thereby ensuring fair returns for farmers and protecting their income.

The MIS focuses on intervening in the market to support the prices of specific commodities when they fall below a predetermined level. The PSS provides price support to farmers by purchasing crops at a minimum support price (MSP) when market prices fall below the MSP.

Market Intervention Scheme and Price Support Scheme (MIS-PSS)

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Farmers producing crops covered under the scheme.
  • State Governments are also involved in implementing the schemes and ensuring farmers' access to price support mechanisms.

Implementation:

  • The MIS is implemented by the Central Government through designated agencies or organizations that purchase the commodities directly from farmers or market yards to stabilize prices.
  • The PSS involves the Central Government buying crops at MSP from farmers when the market prices fall below the MSP, ensuring that farmers receive a guaranteed price for their produce.

Benefits:

  • Price Stabilization: Helps in stabilizing the prices of agricultural commodities and preventing price crashes.
  • Income Support: Ensures that farmers receive fair prices for their produce, thereby protecting their income during periods of market volatility.
  • Market Confidence: Enhances farmers' confidence in the agricultural market by providing a safety net against extreme price fluctuations.

Recent Updates:

  • Periodic reviews and adjustments to the schemes based on market conditions and feedback from stakeholders.
  • Expansion of the range of commodities covered under the schemes to address emerging market issues and farmer needs.

How to Apply:

Farmers can participate in the schemes through their local agricultural offices or through state government agencies responsible for implementing the MIS and PSS. The process typically involves registering with the designated agencies and providing necessary documentation related to their produce and land holdings.
