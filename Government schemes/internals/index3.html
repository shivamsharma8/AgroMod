Overview

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India on February 18, 2016, aimed at providing financial support to farmers in the event of crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. This initiative is part of the government's efforts to promote sustainable agriculture and protect farmers from financial distress.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojna (PMFBY) aims to provide a comprehensive insurance cover against failure of the crop thus helping in stabilising the income of the farmers. The scheme covers all Food & Oilseeds crops and Annual Commercial/Horticultural Crops for which past yield data is available and for which requisite number of crop cutting experiments are being conducted under General Crop Estimation Survey. The scheme is implemented by empanelled general insurance companies. Selection of Implementing Agency is done by the concerned State Government through bidding. The scheme was earlier compulsory for loanee farmers availing crop loan /KCC account for notified crops and voluntary for other others, but has been made voluntary since 2020 when reforms in the scheme were introduced.[2] The scheme is being administered by Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare.

Eligibility Criteria:-

  • PMFBY is available to all farmers including sharecroppers and tenant farmers growing the notified crops in the notified areas.
  • It is compulsory for farmers who have taken a loan for the notified crops under the Kisan Credit Card (KCC) scheme or any other institutional credit.
  • The scheme is voluntary for non-loanee farmers.

Registration Process:-

  • Farmers need to enroll in the scheme by contacting their respective bank, Common Service Centers (CSCs), or through the National Crop Insurance Portal. visit Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana
  • Crop Cutting Experiments (CCEs) are conducted to collect yield data to assess crop losses.
  • Farmers pay their share of the premium, and the government covers the rest.
  • In the event of crop loss, the claims are processed based on yield data obtained from the CCEs or other advanced technologies.

Benefits:-

  • Provides farmers with financial protection against losses due to unforeseen natural events.
  • Encourages the adoption of improved agricultural practices and technologies.
  • : By mitigating the financial risks associated with farming, the scheme aims to reduce instances of farmer suicides due to financial stress.

Recent Updates:-

  • The government has been incorporating drone technology for more accurate and efficient crop loss assessments. This helps in minimizing delays in data collection and claim settlements.
  • Launch of dedicated mobile applications like "Crop Insurance" app to help farmers enroll in the scheme, check their status, and report crop losses. These apps aim to improve transparency and accessibility for farmers.

How to Check Beneficiary Status:-

Farmers can check their beneficiary status by visiting the official PMFBY website or the National Crop Insurance Portal and entering their details, such as Aadhaar number, bank account number, or registered mobile number.
