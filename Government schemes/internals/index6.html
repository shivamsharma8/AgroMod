Formation & Promotion of New 10,000 FPOs

Initiatives by Central Ministries and Departments

Overview

The "Formation & Promotion of 10,000 Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)" scheme is a Central Sector Scheme launched by the Government of India to enhance the collective strength of farmers. The scheme aims to form and promote 10,000 new FPOs over five years, from 2020-21 to 2024-25, with the goal of improving the income levels and livelihood security of small and marginal farmers. FPOs are voluntary organizations formed by primary producers, where members are farmers, milk producers, fishermen, weavers, rural artisans, and craftsmen.

The FPOs are based on the concept of "Cluster-Based Business Organizations" and are intended to be run by farmers and entrepreneurs themselves, ensuring they cater specifically to the needs of their members. The FPOs will provide better bargaining power, access to inputs, technology, credit, and market linkages to their members.

Key Features of the Scheme

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) should consist of small, marginal, and landless farmers.
  • Minimum of 300 members in plain areas and 100 members in hilly and North-Eastern regions.
  • Registered entities, such as Producer Companies under the Companies Act or Cooperative Societies.

Financial Support:

  • Support of up to Rs. 18 lakh per FPO or as decided by the government from time to time.
  • Management cost assistance for the first three years of operation, including office space, salaries of management and personnel, etc.
  • Equity grant of up to Rs. 15 lakh per FPO based on member contribution.
  • Credit guarantee facility up to Rs. 2 crore per FPO under Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE).

Registration Process:

  • Interested farmer groups can apply through their respective state agriculture departments or contact the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD).
  • Required documents include proof of membership, legal registration documents, and a business plan.

Benefits:

  • Provides access to quality inputs and better prices for outputs, reducing the cost of production.
  • Enhances market access and linkages, improving farmers' income and living standards.
  • Facilitates capacity building, technical assistance, and business development services to strengthen FPOs.

Recent Updates:

  • Regular monitoring and evaluation of the scheme to ensure effective implementation and achievement of the objectives.
  • Periodic training programs and workshops conducted for FPO members to build capacities and improve organizational management.

How to Check Application Status:

Applicants can check their status by contacting their state agriculture department or NABARD or by visiting the official portal dedicated to FPO promotion.
