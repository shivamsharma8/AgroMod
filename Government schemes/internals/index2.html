Overview

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a government pension scheme in India aimed at providing social security to small and marginal farmers in their old age. Launched by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare in 2019, it is part of the government’s efforts to support the agricultural sector and ensure the financial well-being of farmers after retirement.

The primary objective of the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana is to provide a regular pension to small and marginal farmers after the age of 60. This pension scheme helps ensure financial stability for farmers in their old age, reducing their dependence on others and improving their quality of life.

Eligibility Criteria:-

  • Age: Farmers between the ages of 18 and 40 are eligible to enroll.
  • Landholding: The scheme is targeted at small and marginal farmers who own cultivable land up to 2 hectares.
  • Exclusions: Institutional landholders, and farmers who are covered under other statutory social security schemes like the National Pension Scheme (NPS), Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) scheme, or Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO), are not eligible

Registration Process:-

  • Enrollment is done through the Common Service Centers (CSCs) across India. Farmers need to visit the nearest CSC with their Aadhaar card and bank passbook.
  • The farmer can also self-register by visiting the official PM-KMY website or through the Ministry of Labour & Employment portal. .

Benefits:-

  • Monthly Pension: Upon reaching the age of 60, enrolled farmers receive a monthly pension of ₹3,000.
  • Family Pension: In case the farmer dies before the retirement age, the spouse can continue in the scheme by paying the remaining contributions. If the farmer dies after the retirement age, the spouse receives 50% of the pension as a family pension. This pension is only applicable to the spouse.

Recent Updates:-

  • PM-KISAN: Provides Rs. 6,000 annually to landholding farmers in three instalments via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). As of August 2024, over Rs. 3.24 lakh crores have been distributed to more than 11 crore beneficiaries
  • PMFBY: The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, launched in 2016, has enrolled 62.60 crore farmer applications and provided claims amounting to over Rs. 1,63,518.70 crore since its inception​(Press Information Bureau).
