National Beekeeping and Honey Mission
(NBHM)

Initiatives by Central Ministries and Departments

Overview

The "National Beekeeping and Honey Mission (NBHM)" is a Central Sector Scheme launched by the Government of India under the Atma Nirbhar Bharat initiative. The mission aims to promote holistic growth of the beekeeping industry, thereby increasing farmer income, generating rural employment, and ensuring nutritional and ecological security. The mission focuses on promoting scientific beekeeping practices to enhance the production and quality of honey and other beehive products.

The NBHM is being implemented through the National Bee Board (NBB) under the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. The scheme promotes beekeeping as an enterprise for small and marginal farmers, tribal communities, and unemployed youth, especially in rural areas, to diversify their income sources and support sustainable agricultural practices.

Key Features of the Scheme

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Individuals and organizations involved in beekeeping, honey production, and allied activities, including farmers, rural youth, and beekeepers' cooperatives.
  • Preference given to small and marginal farmers, women, and socially disadvantaged groups.
  • Registered entities like Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), Self-Help Groups (SHGs), and other beekeeping enterprises.

Financial Support:

  • Provision of financial assistance for setting up beekeeping units, production of high-quality honey, and marketing and branding of honey and other beehive products.
  • Support for capacity building, including training and workshops for beekeepers and other stakeholders.
  • Incentives for developing infrastructure, including honey testing labs, packaging units, and storage facilities.
  • Financial aid for research and development activities related to beekeeping and honey production.

Registration Process:

  • Interested individuals and organizations can register through their respective state agriculture or horticulture departments.
  • Applications can also be submitted via the National Bee Board's official website.
  • Required documents include proof of identity, details of the proposed beekeeping project, and a business plan.

Benefits:

  • Improves crop yields through pollination services provided by bees.
  • Enhances farmers' income through the sale of honey and other beehive products like beeswax, royal jelly, and propolis.
  • Promotes sustainable agricultural practices and helps in environmental conservation.
  • Creates employment opportunities in rural areas, especially for women and marginalized communities.

Recent Updates:

  • Several states have initiated awareness programs and capacity-building workshops to encourage participation in beekeeping activities.
  • Increased focus on developing honey clusters and promoting organic honey production.
  • Regular monitoring and evaluation to ensure the effective implementation of the mission.

How to Check Application Status:

Applicants can check their status by visiting the National Bee Board's official website or contacting their state agriculture or horticulture departments.
